Choosing the Best Change Model

Drag each scenario to the change model that would be the best fit: Lewin, Kotter, or ADKAR.

Tip: You can also click a scenario, then click a model box to place it.

Scenarios

A team is moving from an old workflow to a new standardized process.
An organization-wide transformation requires urgency, communication, and visible short-term wins.
Employees adopting new software need training, practice, and reinforcement.
Leaders want to create a shared vision and change organizational culture.
Employees understand the change but still need motivation to adopt the new behavior.
A company wants a simple framework to help employees unlearn old habits and stabilize new routines.

Change Models

Lewin

Best for clear transitions from a current state to a new stable state.

Kotter

Best for large-scale change requiring urgency, vision, communication, and momentum.

ADKAR

Best for individual adoption, readiness, training, ability, and reinforcement.

Place each scenario under a model, then choose Check Answers.

Answer Explanations

Lewin: Use when the change has a clear current state, transition, and future state that needs to be stabilized.

Kotter: Use when the change is large, complex, and requires urgency, leadership alignment, communication, and momentum.

ADKAR: Use when success depends on individuals adopting new behaviors through awareness, desire, knowledge, ability, and reinforcement.
