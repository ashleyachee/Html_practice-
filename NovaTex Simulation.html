Branching Marketing Simulation

NovaTech Crisis Investigation Dashboard

You are a marketing analyst at NovaTech. Customer sentiment has dropped sharply, social media complaints are spiking, and news outlets are questioning the company’s credibility. Your job is to investigate the evidence, avoid jumping to conclusions, and guide the company toward the right crisis response.

Reputation management Crisis communication timing Transparency vs silence Social listening Trust signals

1. Scenario Introduction

A sudden wave of online complaints is affecting NovaTech’s new cloud-connected home productivity device, NovaHub. Has the brand suffered a real security incident, or is something else driving the reputation drop?

Your role: Review evidence from multiple channels, make investigation choices, interpret incomplete and sometimes misleading data, and recommend the next marketing action.

Marketing Concepts

Social listening: Monitor how narratives spread before they harden into “truth.”
Crisis timing: Slow responses often increase uncertainty and speculation.
Transparency: Acknowledge concern without overclaiming certainty.
Trust signals: Clear updates, FAQs, and visible leadership messaging reduce confusion.
