Interactive Simulation

You are the new change leader at NovaTech Retail.

NovaTech Retail is a mid-sized retailer under pressure. Digital competitors are gaining ground, customer expectations are shifting, internal systems are outdated, and performance is declining. Your role is to assess what is driving change, determine whether the organization is ready, and build a compelling case for action.

This experience is organized into three applied phases:

  • Phase 1: Diagnose the Culture
  • Phase 2: Identify Drivers and Readiness
  • Phase 3: Lead the Change
What you will practice: identifying dominant culture patterns, distinguishing internal and external drivers, recognizing disruptive change, assessing readiness with the McKinsey 7-S model, prioritizing stakeholders, and constructing a strong case for change.

How this works

  • You will move through decision points one screen at a time.
  • Select the strongest response for each situation.
  • You will receive immediate feedback after each decision.
  • Your simulation performance will be summarized before a 10-question quiz.
  • The quiz presents one question at a time and includes immediate feedback.

Tip: Focus on evidence, alignment, stakeholder impact, and the practical realities of leading change.

Introduction

Your first week at NovaTech Retail

You have been hired to help NovaTech Retail respond to growing disruption. The executive team knows change is necessary, but there is disagreement about what is causing the problem, how ready the organization is, and how to bring people with them.

What you know so far

Business context

Store traffic is down, margins are tightening, and digital competitors are offering faster, more personalized service.

Operational context

Legacy systems are slowing decisions, creating friction for employees, and limiting the customer experience.

Leadership context

Executives talk about innovation, but employees report mixed signals about what is really valued and rewarded.

Your task

Work through each phase and make evidence-based decisions about culture, readiness, leadership, and stakeholder engagement.

  • Interpret signals from the organization carefully.
  • Look for alignment between strategy, structure, systems, and behavior.
  • Think like a change leader: not only about control, but about coordination, influence, and orchestration.

Key takeaways

  • Culture shapes whether change messages feel credible or contradictory.
  • External disruption creates urgency, but internal alignment determines readiness.
  • Leadership is not only about direction; it is also about engagement, consistency, and partnership.
  • A strong case for change explains why change is necessary, what happens without it, and how stakeholders are affected.
