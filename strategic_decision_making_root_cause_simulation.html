Northstar Health Strategic Decision-Making Dashboard

You are part of the strategy team at Northstar Health, a regional hospital system. Patient satisfaction has been declining, appointment wait times are rising, and patients are reporting breakdowns in care coordination across departments. Leadership initially believes staffing shortages are the main issue. Your job is to review the evidence, identify the most likely root cause, and help the organization choose a response that is both strategic and cost-effective.

Root cause analysis Cost-benefit thinking Decision-making frameworks Operational evidence Strategic choice

1. Scenario Introduction

Teams across Northstar Health are hearing the same complaints: long delays, confusing handoffs between departments, and missed expectations about appointment timing. A new scheduling platform was introduced recently, and now leadership wants to know what the real problem is.

Your role: Review the evidence, identify the strongest root cause, compare the likely value of response options, and determine which decision-making approach leadership should use.

Patient Feedback

Appointment Delays Patients say visits are starting later than expected, especially when multiple departments are involved.
Care Coordination Patients describe confusion about handoffs between registration, imaging, outpatient clinics, and follow-up care.
Communication Some patients say the instructions they received from one department did not match what happened in another.
Staffing Perception Patients sometimes assume understaffing is the issue, but the complaints focus more on process breakdowns than on staff behavior.

Operations Data

Scheduling Accuracy Appointment mismatches increased after the new scheduling platform was introduced.
Department Variation Satisfaction declines are steepest in service lines that rely on multiple department handoffs.
Staffing Levels Staffing has remained relatively stable across the same period, with no sudden drop matching the complaint spike.
Throughput Pattern Delays are concentrated where scheduling handoffs and coordination steps are most complex.

Response Options

Hire More Staff This is a visible response, but it is expensive and may not solve the process problem if coordination is the real issue.
Fix the Scheduling and Coordination Process This requires operational review, training, and system adjustments, but it addresses the pattern most strongly linked to declining satisfaction.
Launch a Patient Communication Campaign This may improve messaging, but it does not correct breakdowns in handoffs, timing, or scheduling accuracy.
Redesign Waiting Areas This may improve comfort, but the evidence suggests the main issue is process reliability rather than physical space.

Key Strategy Concepts

Root cause analysis: Identify the underlying source of the problem before choosing a solution.
Cost-benefit thinking: Compare likely value, tradeoffs, and resource use before acting.
Decision-making frameworks: Use structured logic instead of jumping to the most visible response.
Evidence patterns: Time trends and linked variables can reveal stronger explanations than assumptions.
Strategic fit: The best option aligns the cause, the response, and the expected outcome.
