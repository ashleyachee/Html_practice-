Design an Automated Email Campaign

Learning Objective: Practice marketing automation concepts by designing a 4-email nurture campaign and identifying explicit and implicit marketing data.

Scenario

A company offers a free downloadable guide about improving digital marketing performance.

When visitors submit a form to download the guide, the marketing team wants to nurture the lead automatically and encourage the visitor to schedule a product demo.

Your job is to design the automation campaign.

Feedback

Best Practice Reminder

A strong nurture sequence moves from delivery, to learning, to proof, to conversion. It also combines form data with behavior data so the team can personalize follow-up and measure engagement.

Example Strong Campaign

This is one example of a strong 4-email automated nurture sequence.

Email 1 – Welcome / Resource Delivery

Timing: Immediately after form submission

Purpose: Deliver the guide and welcome the subscriber

CTA: Download the guide

Headline: Your Guide Is Here — Welcome!

Email 2 – Educational Nurture

Timing: 2 days later

Purpose: Provide additional educational content

CTA: Read a related article

Headline: 3 Tips to Improve Your Digital Marketing Strategy

Email 3 – Proof / Value Building

Timing: 4 days later

Purpose: Share a case study or success story

CTA: Watch a demo video

Headline: See the Results: A Customer Success Story

Email 4 – Conversion / Demo Invitation

Timing: 7 days later

Purpose: Encourage scheduling a demo

CTA: Schedule a demo

Headline: Ready for the Next Step? Schedule Your Demo
