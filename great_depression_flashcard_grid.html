Key Financial Regulation Acts

Select each card to review the purpose and impact of major financial regulations.

Securities Act of 1933
Requires companies issuing securities to the public for the first time to disclose important financial information and prohibits fraud in the issuance of securities.
Securities Act of 1934
Created the SEC and established rules for ongoing financial disclosure and regulation of securities markets.
Sarbanes-Oxley Act (2002)
Strengthened financial reporting requirements, required internal controls, and increased accountability for corporate executives and auditors.
Public Company Accounting Oversight Board (PCAOB)
Oversees audits of public companies to ensure accurate and independent financial reporting—often called the “auditor of the auditors.”
Dodd-Frank Act (2008)
Aimed to improve financial stability by reducing risky financial practices following the 2007–2008 financial crisis.
