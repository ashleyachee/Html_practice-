Lean vs. Six Sigma

Lean and Six Sigma are improvement methods that help organizations work better. Lean focuses on reducing waste. Six Sigma focuses on reducing defects and variation.

Simple Comparison

Lean

Removes waste and improves flow.

Six Sigma

Reduces errors and inconsistency.

Both

Improve quality, efficiency, and outcomes.

Lean

Use Lean when work takes too long or includes unnecessary steps.
  • Removes waste
  • Improves speed and flow
  • Helps teams focus on value for the customer
Six Sigma

Use Six Sigma when errors, defects, or inconsistent results are a problem.
  • Reduces variation
  • Improves consistency
  • Uses data to identify and solve quality problems
When to Use Each

The right approach depends on the problem being solved.
  • Lean: Use when the process is slow, wasteful, or inefficient.
  • Six Sigma: Use when results are inconsistent or quality problems occur.
  • Lean Six Sigma: Use both to improve efficiency and quality together.

Main Takeaway

Lean helps organizations do work with less waste. Six Sigma helps organizations do work with fewer errors. Together, they support continuous improvement.
