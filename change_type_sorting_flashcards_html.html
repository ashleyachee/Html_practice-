Types of Change Sorting Activity

Read each scenario and choose the type of change it best represents. Use the guide below if you need a reminder.

Instructions: For each card, select one type of change: anticipatory, reactive, crisis, disruptive, or evolutionary. When you are finished, select Check Answers to receive feedback.

Anticipatory Change

Planned in advance based on trends, forecasts, or expected opportunities.

Reactive Change

Occurs after a problem, pressure, or performance issue has already appeared.

Crisis Change

Requires urgent action because a serious threat or emergency is unfolding.

Disruptive Change

Challenges existing ways of working, often because of major innovation or market shifts.

Evolutionary Change

Gradual improvement over time through small adjustments and continuous learning.
