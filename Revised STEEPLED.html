STEEPLED Analysis

Click each element to explore external forces that can shape business decisions.

Explore the eight elements

Use each panel to review the core idea, examples, and practical questions.

S

Social

Values, lifestyles, attitudes, and behaviors.
Core idea:

Social forces shape demand, brand expectations, workplace culture, and consumer preferences.

What to look for

  • Changing values
  • Work-life expectations
  • Lifestyle trends

Simple example

A company may offer healthier or more flexible options as customer preferences shift.

Ask this

How are behaviors or values changing customer expectations?

T

Technological

Innovation, digital tools, and new systems.
Core idea:

Technology can change how organizations create value, communicate, produce, analyze data, and serve customers.

What to look for

  • Automation and AI
  • Software platforms
  • Cybersecurity

Simple example

A retailer may use chatbots or dashboards to improve service and efficiency.

Ask this

What technologies could improve or disrupt the business model?

E

Economic

Financial conditions, costs, spending, and growth.
Core idea:

Economic conditions affect purchasing power, pricing, hiring, investment, and stability.

What to look for

  • Inflation
  • Interest rates
  • Consumer confidence

Simple example

During inflation, costs may rise while customers become more price-sensitive.

Ask this

How might economic conditions affect demand or costs?

E

Environmental

Sustainability, resources, climate, and waste.
Core idea:

Environmental factors include climate risks, resource use, waste, and sustainability expectations.

What to look for

  • Climate disruptions
  • Resource use
  • Waste reduction

Simple example

A manufacturer may redesign packaging to reduce waste.

Ask this

What environmental pressures affect reputation or planning?

P

Political

Government priorities, policy, and stability.
Core idea:

Political forces include policy shifts, funding priorities, taxation, trade, and stability.

What to look for

  • Policy changes
  • Trade conditions
  • Public funding

Simple example

A healthcare company may adjust strategy based on government funding priorities.

Ask this

Are political decisions creating risks or opportunities?

L

Legal

Laws, regulations, and compliance requirements.
Core idea:

Legal factors shape hiring, contracts, data use, product standards, advertising, and compliance.

What to look for

  • Employment law
  • Privacy rules
  • Industry compliance

Simple example

A business may change data practices to meet privacy requirements.

Ask this

What legal requirements could raise costs or risk?

E

Ethical

Fairness, responsibility, integrity, and trust.
Core idea:

Ethical factors involve doing what is responsible, fair, and trustworthy for stakeholders.

What to look for

  • Transparency
  • Responsible sourcing
  • Stakeholder trust

Simple example

An organization may avoid a tactic that feels misleading or harmful.

Ask this

Would stakeholders see this as fair and responsible?

D

Demographic

Population characteristics, labor, and market needs.
Core idea:

Demographic factors examine age, income, education, location, and population change.

What to look for

  • Age distribution
  • Income levels
  • Migration patterns

Simple example

An aging population may increase demand for healthcare or accessibility-focused products.

Ask this

How might population shifts change customer or workforce needs?

Main takeaway: STEEPLED helps managers scan the external environment so they can identify opportunities, risks, and strategic priorities.
