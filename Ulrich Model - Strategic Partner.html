The Ulrich Model of HR: Strategic Partner

The Ulrich Model describes major ways HR supports an organization. This visual highlights the strategic partner role, which focuses on helping the organization connect people decisions to business goals.

Big idea: A strategic partner does more than handle HR tasks. This role helps leaders plan ahead, align talent with organizational needs, improve performance, and support long-term success.

Where strategic partner fits in the model

The Ulrich Model is often shown as a set of connected HR roles. In this simplified view, each role supports the organization in a different way.

Strategic Partner

Aligns people, talent, and HR plans with organizational strategy and future goals.

Focus: business success through people

Administrative Expert

Builds clear, efficient HR processes, policies, and systems.

Focus: consistency and operations

Employee Champion

Supports employee needs, fairness, experience, and engagement.

Focus: people support and advocacy

Change Agent

Helps the organization adapt to change, growth, and new ways of working.

Focus: transition and improvement

In practice, HR professionals may do work from all four areas. The strategic partner role stands out because it links HR decisions to the organization’s mission, priorities, and future direction.

Align HR with strategy

A strategic partner makes sure HR work supports what the organization is trying to achieve. Instead of asking only, “What HR task needs to be done?” this role asks, “How can HR help the organization move forward?”

Main duties

  • Understand organizational goals and priorities
  • Connect HR plans to growth, quality, service, or innovation goals
  • Design HR practices that support long-term direction

What this can look like

  • Updating hiring priorities based on business needs
  • Creating development plans for future roles
  • Reviewing whether HR programs support strategy
Example: A company wants to expand into new markets. HR helps identify what skills employees will need, what roles must be filled, and how training can support the expansion.

Plan future talent needs

Strategic partners look ahead. They help the organization think about who it will need, what employees must be able to do, and where talent gaps may appear.

Main duties

  • Study workforce trends and future staffing needs
  • Identify skill gaps and succession needs
  • Support workforce planning and talent pipelines

What this can look like

  • Forecasting retirements or turnover
  • Preparing internal employees for leadership roles
  • Planning recruiting strategies for hard-to-fill jobs
Example: An organization knows several managers may retire in the next two years. HR helps create a succession plan so future leaders can be prepared before those roles become vacant.

Improve performance

Strategic partners look at how HR systems affect results. They help build conditions where employees can perform well and where the organization can meet its goals.

Main duties

  • Review whether HR practices support strong performance
  • Use people data to guide improvements
  • Support learning, accountability, and goal alignment

What this can look like

  • Improving onboarding to reduce early turnover
  • Revising performance goals to match team priorities
  • Using survey or retention data to guide action
Example: If a department has high turnover, HR does more than replace staff. A strategic partner studies the cause, identifies patterns, and works with leaders on better solutions.

Advise leaders

Strategic partners serve as thought partners for managers and executives. They bring HR knowledge into important organizational decisions and help leaders think through people impact.

Main duties

  • Guide leaders on workforce and organizational decisions
  • Bring a people perspective into planning conversations
  • Help balance business goals with employee impact

What this can look like

  • Advising on team redesign or restructuring
  • Helping leaders prepare for growth or change
  • Supporting decisions with HR data and trends
Example: Before launching a major change, leaders meet with HR to discuss staffing, communication, training, and employee concerns. That is strategic partner work.
Why this matters: The strategic partner role shows that HR is not only administrative. It plays a direct role in helping organizations plan, adapt, and succeed through people.
