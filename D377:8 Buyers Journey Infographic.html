The buyer’s journey outlines the steps buyers take to make a purchasing decision. Use the accordion below to explore common digital touchpoints and how companies engage prospective customers at each stage.

What buyers are doing

At this stage, buyers are noticing a need, problem, product category, or brand. The goal is to capture attention and make the brand easy to remember.

Common touchpoints

PROutdoor AdsPrint AdsOnline AdsRadio/TVWord-of-Mouth
Marketing focus: Make the audience notice the brand and understand why it may be relevant.

What buyers are doing

At this stage, buyers are gathering information. Helpful content should answer questions, explain value, and make it easier to compare possible solutions.

Common touchpoints

BlogsVideoPaid ContentSocial Media AdsEmailSearch
Marketing focus: Provide useful information that builds trust and helps buyers evaluate options.

What buyers are doing

At this stage, buyers are ready to act. Companies should reduce friction, make the next step clear, and support a smooth transaction.

Common touchpoints

Store PurchaseOnline PurchaseApp Purchase
Marketing focus: Make the purchase path simple, clear, and aligned with customer expectations.

What buyers are doing

At this stage, companies continue the relationship after the purchase. Support, rewards, and useful updates can encourage customers to return.

Common touchpoints

Customer SupportRewards ProgramNewsletter/Blog Subscription
Marketing focus: Strengthen the relationship so customers have a reason to keep engaging with the brand.

What buyers are doing

At this stage, satisfied customers may influence others. Reviews, testimonials, and social conversations can extend the brand’s reach.

Common touchpoints

ForumsSocial Media MentionsSurveysReviewsTestimonials
Marketing focus: Encourage and learn from customer voices while making positive experiences visible.

Why This Matters

Effective digital marketing meets buyers where they are. A buyer who is just becoming aware of a brand needs different content than a buyer who is ready to purchase or recommend the brand to others.
